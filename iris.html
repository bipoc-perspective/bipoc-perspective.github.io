    Iris attended an academically rigorous public school where at least half of the students in their school took mostly STEM AP classes. They were mostly white and east asian and sometimes topics of race were spoken about as though it was theoretical. Due to the muted presence of other POC students, experience was less often brought into the classroom when talking about race. As a non-binary queer autistic activist, they did not fit in with the model student at the school and chose Wellesley hoping to find that community. Someday, they want to be making a change in the world.

    Educational justice for Iris looks like a world where students are supported to thrive, not just survive.
